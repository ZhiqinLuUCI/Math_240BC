Section 7.6 More about elliptic regularity

Let \(M\) be a compact Riemannian manifold and let \(\phi\in \mathcal H^p_1(M)\) be a \(p\)-form. We say \(\phi\) is a weak solution of the equation

\begin{equation*} (\Delta+I)\phi=\psi \end{equation*}

for \(\psi\) a \(C^\infty\) \(p\)-form, if

  1. \(\phi\in \mathcal H^p_1(M)\text{;}\)

  2. for any \(\eta\) a smooth \(p\)-form, we have

    \begin{equation*} (\phi,(\Delta+I)\eta)=(\psi,\eta)\text{.} \end{equation*}

In this section, we prove the following result.

Theorem 7.6.1.

Let \(\phi\) be a weak solution of the above equation, then \(\phi\) must be smooth.

Proof.

Let \(\rho\) be a smooth function. Then by the Weitzenb√∂ck formula, we have

\begin{equation} (\Delta+I)(\rho\eta)=\rho(\Delta+I)\eta+J(\eta)\text{,}\tag{7.6.1} \end{equation}

where \(J\) is some first order differential operator (depending on \(\rho\)) on the space of \(p\)-forms. Thus we have

\begin{equation} (\rho\phi, (\Delta+I)\eta)=(\psi,\rho\eta)-(\phi,J(\eta))\tag{7.6.2} \end{equation}

for any smooth form \(\eta\in\Lambda^p(M)\text{.}\)

Now we specify our choice of \(\rho\text{.}\) Let \(x\in M\) be a fixed point. We choose a local normal coordinate system \((U,(x_1,\cdots,x_n))\text{.}\) We choose a smooth function \(\rho\) such that the support of \(\rho\) is within \(U\text{,}\) and is constant \(1\) in a smaller neighborhood \(V\) of \(x\) such that \(\bar V\subset U\text{.}\) Without loss of generality, we may assume that \(\rho\phi,\eta\) are \(p\)-forms of a torus \(T^n\text{.}\)

Let \((\,\,,\,\,)_0\) be the inner product on \(\Lambda^p(T^n)\) induced by the flat Riemannian metric and let \(||||_0\) be the corresponding norm. Then there is a constant \(C>0\) such that

\begin{equation*} C^{-1}||||_0\leq ||||\leq C||||_0\text{.} \end{equation*}

In fact, since we choose the local normal coordinate system, we may assume that near \(x\text{,}\) the Riemannian metrics are very close.

Let \(A\) be the zero-th differential operator such that

\begin{equation*} (\rho\phi,(\Delta_0+I)\eta)_0=(\rho\phi, A(\Delta_0+I)\eta)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Let \(P\) be the operator

\begin{equation*} P=A(\Delta_0+I)-(\Delta+I)\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(\Delta_0\) is the Laplacian with respect to the flat metric of \(T^n\text{.}\) Then we have

\begin{equation*} (\rho\phi,(\Delta_0+I)\eta)_0=(\rho\phi, P\eta)+(\psi,\rho\eta)-(\phi, J(\eta))\text{.} \end{equation*}

Let

\begin{equation*} \rho\phi\sim\sum_{\xi\in\mathbb Z^n}a_\xi e^{i\langle x,\xi\rangle} \end{equation*}

be the Fourier expansion of \(\rho\phi\text{,}\) where \(a_\xi\) are \(p\)-forms. Let \(R,s\) be large real numbers and let

\begin{equation*} \eta_R=\sum_{\|\xi\|\lt R}a_\xi e^{i\langle x,\xi\rangle}(1+||\xi||^2)^{s} \end{equation*}

Since

\begin{equation*} \Delta_0=-\sum_{j=1}^n\frac{\pa^2}{\pa x_j^2}\text{,} \end{equation*}

We have

\begin{equation*} \Delta_0 e^{i\langle x,\xi\rangle}=\|\xi\|^2e^{i\langle x,\xi\rangle}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Then we have

\begin{equation} \sum_{\|\xi\|\lt R}|a_\xi|^2(1+\|\xi\|^2)^{s+1}=(\eta_R,(\Delta_0+I)\eta_R)_0\text{.}\tag{7.6.3} \end{equation}

Let \(\tilde\Delta\) be the raw Laplacian. Let

\begin{equation*} \tilde P=A(\Delta_0+I)-(\tilde\Delta+I)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Then we have

\begin{equation*} (\rho\phi, P\eta)=(\rho\phi,\tilde P\eta)+(\rho\phi, E\eta)\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(E\) is the zero-th operator in the Weitzenb√∂ck formula.

We claim that

  1. \(|(\rho\phi, \tilde P\eta_R)|\leq\eps\|\phi\|^2_{\mathcal H_{(s+1)/2}}\) for \(0\lt \eps\ll1\text{;}\)

  2. \(|(\rho\phi, E\eta_R)|\leq C\|\phi\|^2_{\mathcal H_{s/2}}\text{;}\)

  3. \(|(\psi, \rho\eta_R)|\leq C \|\phi\|_{\mathcal H_{s/2}}\text{;}\)

  4. \(|(\phi, J(\eta_R))|\leq C\|\phi\|^2_{\mathcal H_{s/2+1/4}}\text{.}\)

We will postpone the proof of the above claim in the following. Now let's assume it is correct. Letting \(R\to\infty\) in¬†(7.6.3), we have

\begin{equation*} \|\phi\|^2_{\mathcal H_{(s+1)/2}}\leq \eps \|\phi\|^2_{\mathcal H_{(s+1)/2}}+C\|\phi\|^2_{\mathcal H_{s/2}}+C\|\phi\|_{\mathcal H_{s/2}}+C\|\phi\|^2_{\mathcal H_{s/2+1/4}}\text{.} \end{equation*}

This by induction, \(\phi\in \mathcal H_s(M)\) for any \(s\text{,}\) and hence by the Sobolev Lemma, \(\phi\) has to be smooth.

It remains to prove the claim. We shall only prove (1), the others begin similar.

\(\tilde P\) is a differential operator with small coefficients. Let

\begin{equation*} \tilde P=a_{ij}\frac{\pa^2}{\pa x_i\pa x_j}+b_i\frac{\pa}{\pa x_i}+c\text{.} \end{equation*}

Then we have

\begin{equation*} \tilde \eta_R=\sum_{\|\xi\|\lt R}a_\xi e^{i\langle x,\xi\rangle}(1+\|\xi\|^2)^s\cdot F(x,\xi)\text{,} \end{equation*}

where

\begin{equation*} F(x,\xi)=-a_{ij}\xi_i\xi_j+\sqrt{-1}b_i\xi_i+c\text{.} \end{equation*}

We write

\begin{equation*} \overline{(\rho\phi,\tilde P\eta_R)}=\sum_{\tilde\xi\in\mathbb Z^n} \sum_{\|\xi\|\lt R}\bar a_{\tilde\xi} \,a_\xi (1+\|\xi\|^2)^s\, \int_M F(x,\xi) e^{i\langle x,(\xi-\tilde\xi)\rangle}\text{.} \end{equation*}

By the Riemann-Lebesgue lemma, we have

\begin{equation*} \left|\int_M F(x,\xi) e^{i\langle x,(\xi-\tilde\xi)\rangle}\right|\leq C\frac{1+\|\xi\|^2}{\|\xi-\tilde\xi\|^K} \end{equation*}

for any \(K>0\text{.}\) Below we shall take \(K=(s+1)/2\) and use the inequality that

\begin{equation*} (1+\|\xi\|^2)\cdot\|\xi-\tilde\xi\|\leq C\,(1+\|\tilde\xi\|^2)\text{.} \end{equation*}

and this completes the proof of the claim.
