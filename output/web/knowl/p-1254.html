Then from (7.3.2), \(\eta_0\) is harmonic, i.e., \(\Delta\eta_0=0\text{.}\) Thus the solution of the problem (5.3.2) must be harmonic. For any element \(\eta\) in a fixed cohomologyical class \([a]\text{,}\) we have the following Hodge decomposition

\begin{equation*} \eta=\eta_0+d \eta_1\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(\eta_0\) is the harmonic form, and \(\eta_1\) is a \(p-1\) form. In general, for arbitrary \(p\) form \(\eta\text{,}\) we have the following Hodge decomposition:

\begin{equation*} \eta=\eta_0+d\xi_1+\delta\xi_2\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(\eta_0\text{,}\) \(\xi_1\text{,}\) and \(\xi_2\) are smooth \(p\text{,}\) \(p-1\text{,}\) and \(p+1\) forms, respectively, and \(\eta_0\) is harmonic.
