We use the \(R_t, U_t\) to denote the corresponding families of operators with respect to the functions \(R(x,y,t)\) and \(U(x,y,t)\text{,}\) respectively. For any \(k\geq 1\text{,}\) define the operator

\begin{equation*} Q_t^k=\int_{t\Delta_k} U_{t-t_k}R_{t_k-t_{k-1}}\cdots R_{t_2-t_1}R_{t_1} dt_1\cdots dt_k\text{,} \end{equation*}

and \(Q_t^0=U_t\text{.}\) Let

\begin{equation*} R^{(k)} (s)=\int_{s\Delta_{k-1}}R_{s-t_{k-1}}\cdots R_{t_2-t_1} R_{t_1} dt_1\cdots dt_{k-1}\text{,} \end{equation*}

and \(R^{(0)}(s)=0\text{.}\) Since the derivative of the integral of the form

\begin{equation*} \int_0^t a(t-s) b(s) ds \end{equation*}

is equal to

\begin{equation*} \int_0^t\frac{da}{dt}(t-s) b(s) ds+a(0) b(t)\text{,} \end{equation*}

we have

\begin{equation*} \left(\frac{\pa}{\pa t}-\Delta\right )Q_t^k=R^{(k+1)} (t)+R^{(k)} (t)\text{.} \end{equation*}
