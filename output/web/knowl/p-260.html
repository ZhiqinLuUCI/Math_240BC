Let \(M\) be a compact manifold without boundary (we call such a manifold closed). Then by the Hodge theorem, for any function \(f\) such that

\begin{equation*} \int_M f=0\text{,} \end{equation*}

we have

\begin{equation*} \int_M|\nabla f|^2\geq C\int_M f^2 \end{equation*}

for a positive constant \(C\text{.}\) For manifold with boundary, we have similar versions of Poincar√© inequalities with respect to the boundary conditions.
