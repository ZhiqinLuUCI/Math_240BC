We prove that the volume of \(M\) grows uniformly and sub-exponentially. To see this, we observe that for any point \((x)\)

\begin{equation*} v(B_1(x)) \geqslant \frac{C}{r(x)^\alpha} \end{equation*}

for some constant \(C > 0\text{.}\) On the other hand, if \(\alpha > 1\text{,}\) the volume of manifold is finite. Thus

\begin{equation*} v(B_r(x)) \leqslant C \leqslant e^{\varepsilon r}\frac{C(\varepsilon)}{r^\alpha} \end{equation*}

for any \(r \gg 0\text{.}\)
