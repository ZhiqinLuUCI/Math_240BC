Proof

There are many fancy proofs of the above result. The proof provided below is the best one (and can be made rigorous).

Let \(S_k(M)\) be the vector spaces generated by all dimension \(k\) submanifolds of \(M\) with boundaries. The map

\begin{equation*} \pa: S_k(M)\to S_{k-1}(M) \end{equation*}

sends those submanifolds into their boundaries. Obviously, \(\pa^2=0\text{.}\) That is, the boundary of the boundary of a submanifold has no boundary. We can define the homology groups as

\begin{equation*} H_k(M)=\frac{\{V\mid \pa V=0, V\in S_k(M)\}}{\{\pa W\mid W\in S_{k+1}(M)\}} \end{equation*}

for all \(k\geq 0\text{.}\) We assume that

\begin{equation*} H_{sing}^k(M)=(H_k(M))^*\text{,} \end{equation*}

which is a combinatorial result and the proof can be found in any book in algebraic topology.

There is a natural non-degenerated coupling between \(\Lambda^k(M)\) and \(S_k(M)\) by the integration

\begin{equation*} \Lambda^k(M)\times S_k(M)\to \mathbb R,\qquad (\omega,V)\mapsto \int_V\omega\text{.} \end{equation*}

By the Stokes theorem, the above coupling descends to the map

\begin{equation*} H^k_{DR}(M)\times H_k(M)\to\mathbb R\text{.} \end{equation*}

To complete the proof, we need to prove that the above coupling is non-degenerated. What we need to prove is that, for any \(p\)-form \(\omega\) such that \(d\omega=0\) and for any \(p\)-dimensional submanifold \(K\) without boundary \(\int_K\omega=0\text{,}\) then there is a \(p-1\) form \(\eta\) such that \(\omega=d\eta\text{.}\) Let \(U,V\) be two open sets and suppose that the theorem is proved for \(U,V\text{.}\) Let \(\omega=d\eta_1\) on \(U\) and \(\omega=d\eta_2\) on \(V\text{.}\) Then on \(U\cap V\text{,}\) \(d(\eta_1-\eta_2)=0\text{.}\) We need to prove that for any \(p-1\) dimensional submanifold \(L\) in \(U\cap V\text{,}\) if \(\int _L(\eta_1-\eta_2)=0\text{,}\) then there is a \(\xi\) such that \(\eta_1-\eta_2=d\xi\text{.}\) Thus \(\eta_1-d\xi=\eta_2\) provides the required solution.
