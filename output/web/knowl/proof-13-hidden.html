Let \(\Delta_2\) be another self-adjoint extension of \(\Delta\text{.}\) Then for \(\phi\in {\mathcal Dom}(\bar\Delta)\) the functional

\begin{equation*} \psi\mapsto(\phi,\Delta\psi)+(\phi,\psi)=(\Delta_2\phi,\psi)+(\phi,\psi) \end{equation*}

for \(\psi\in\Lambda_0^p(M)\) is bounded under the norm \(\|\|_1\text{.}\) By Proposition 2.2.5, it is also bounded under the norm

\begin{equation*} \sqrt{Q(\cdot,\cdot)+(\cdot,\cdot)}\text{.} \end{equation*}

By the Riesz representation theorem, there exists an element \(\mu\in H^1(M)\) such that

\begin{equation*} (\phi,\Delta\psi)+(\phi,\psi)=Q(\mu,\psi)+(\mu,\psi)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Therefore \(\phi=\psi\in H^1(M)\text{.}\) Since \(M\) is complete, By Theorem 2.2.3, \(\phi\in H_0^1(M)\text{.}\) For any \(\psi\in\Lambda_0^p(M)\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} (\Delta_2\phi,\psi)=(\phi,\Delta\psi)=Q(\phi,\psi) \end{equation*}

and by taking limit, the above equality is also true for any \(\psi\in H_0^1(M)\text{.}\) Thus we prove

\begin{equation*} {\mathcal Dom}\,(\Delta_2)\subset {\mathcal Dom}\,(\bar\Delta)\text{.} \end{equation*}

On the other hands, since both \(\bar\Delta\) and \(\Delta_2\) are self-adjoint, we have

\begin{equation*} {\mathcal Dom}\,(\bar\Delta)={\mathcal Dom}\,(\bar\Delta^*)\subset{\mathcal Dom}\,(\Delta_2^*)={\mathcal Dom}\,(\Delta_2)\text{,} \end{equation*}

and thus we prove

\begin{equation*} {\mathcal Dom}\,(\bar\Delta)={\mathcal Dom}\,(\Delta_2)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Thus \(\Delta_2=\bar\Delta\text{,}\) and the extension is unique.
