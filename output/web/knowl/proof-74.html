Proof

Let \(p\in\Omega[x_0,u(x_0)]\text{.}\) Then by definition

\begin{equation*} u(x_0)+p\cdot (x-x_0)\geq\hat u(x)\geq 0\text{.} \end{equation*}

Define

\begin{equation*} \lambda_0=\inf\{\lambda\mid\lambda+p\cdot (x-x_0)\geq u(x),\forall x\in\bar\Omega\}\text{.} \end{equation*}

By the continuity of \(u\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} \lambda_0+p\cdot (x-x_0)\geq u(x),\qquad\forall x\in\bar\Omega\text{,} \end{equation*}

and there is a \(\xi\) such that

\begin{equation*} \lambda_0+p\cdot (\xi-x_0)=u(\xi)\text{.} \end{equation*}

  1. if \(\lambda_0=u(x_0)\text{,}\) then \(x_0\in\Gamma_u^+, p\in\chi(\Gamma_u^+)\text{;}\)

  2. if \(\lambda_0>u(x_0)\text{,}\) then \(\xi\not\in\pa\Omega\text{,}\) otherwise

    \begin{equation*} u(\xi)>u(x_0)+p\cdot (\xi-x_0)\geq 0 \end{equation*}

    contradicting to \(u\leq 0\) on \(\pa\Omega\text{.}\) Since we have

    \begin{equation*} u(\xi)+p\cdot (x-\xi)\geq u(x), \forall x\in\bar\Omega\text{,} \end{equation*}

    where \(\xi\in\Gamma_u^+\) and \(p\in\chi(\Gamma_u^+)\text{.}\)
