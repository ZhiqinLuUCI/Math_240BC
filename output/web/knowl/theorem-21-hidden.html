Let \(X\) be a complete Riemannian manifold of dimension \(n\text{.}\) Let

\begin{equation*} { Ric} (X) \geq - (n - 1) K\text{.} \end{equation*}

Let \(N\) be an \(n\)-dimensional simply connected space form of constant sectional curvature \(-K\text{.}\) Let \(\rho_M, \rho_N\) be the distance functions to fixed reference points, respectively. If \(x \in X\) and \(y \in N\) such that

\begin{equation*} \rho_M (x) = \rho _N (y)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Then in the sense of distribution, we have

\begin{equation*} \Delta \rho _M (x) \leq \Delta \rho _N (y)\text{.} \end{equation*}
