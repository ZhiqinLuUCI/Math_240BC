Theorem 4.1.3.

Let \(M\) be a compact Riemannian manifold with boundary, Ric\((M) \geq 0\text{.}\) If \(\partial M \neq \emptyset\text{,}\) we assume that \(\partial M\) is convex. In this case, we assume that \(u (x, t)\) satisfies the Neumann boundary.

\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial u}{\partial n} = 0 \end{equation*}

on \(\partial M x (0, \infty)\text{,}\) where \(\frac{\partial }{\partial n}\) is the outer normal direction. Then on \(M x (0, \infty)\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} \frac{|\nabla u|^2}{u^2} - \frac{ut}{u} \leq \frac{n}{2t}\text{.} \end{equation*}
