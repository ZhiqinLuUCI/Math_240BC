Remark 3.1.4.

The Bochner formula gives another proof of Â (3.1.1). Let \(f\) be a smooth function. Then we have

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{2} \Delta |\nabla f| ^2 = |\nabla ^2 f |^2 + \frac{\partial }{\partial r} \Delta f + { Ric} ( \nabla f, \nabla f )\text{.} \end{equation*}

If we specialize \(f\) to be \(r\text{,}\) then \(|\nabla r |^2 = 1\) and \(\Delta |\nabla r| ^2 \equiv 0\text{.}\) On the other hand

\begin{equation*} | \nabla ^2 f |^2 \geq \sum_i f_{ii}^2 \geq \frac{1}{n-1} \left( \sum f_{ii} \right) ^2 = \frac{1}{n-1} ( \Delta f ) ^2\text{,} \end{equation*}

and the inequality follows.
