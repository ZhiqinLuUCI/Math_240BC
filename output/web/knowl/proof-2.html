Proof

Let \((x_1,\cdots,x_n)\) be a local coordinate system, and let \(\Gamma_{ij}^k\) be the Christoffel symbols. We then have

\begin{equation*} \nabla_{\frac{\pa}{\pa x_k}}\frac{\pa}{\pa x_i}=\Gamma_{ki}^\ell\frac{\pa}{\pa x_\ell} \end{equation*}

and

\begin{equation*} \nabla_{\frac{\pa}{\pa x_k}}dx_j=-\Gamma_{k\ell}^jdx_\ell \end{equation*}

Thus we have

\begin{equation*} \nabla_{\frac{\pa}{\pa x_k}} g_{ij}dx_idx_j =\frac{\pa g_{ij}}{\pa x_k}dx_idx_j-g_{ij}\Gamma_{k\ell}^i dx_\ell dx_j-g_{ij}\Gamma_{k\ell}^j dx_i dx_\ell=0\text{.} \end{equation*}
