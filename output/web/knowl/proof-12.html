Proof

For any \(\phi,\psi\in{\mathcal Dom}(\bar\Delta)\text{,}\) \((\bar\Delta\phi,\psi)=Q(\psi,\phi)=(\phi,\bar\Delta\psi)\) is a bounded functional. Thus \(\phi\in {\mathcal Dom}(\bar\Delta^*)\text{.}\) On the other hand, if \(\phi\in {\mathcal Dom}(\Delta^*)\text{,}\) then the functional \(\psi\mapsto (\Delta\psi,\phi)\) is bounded. By the Riesz representation theorem, there is a unique \(f\in L^2(\Lambda^p(M))\) such that \((\bar\Delta\psi,\phi)=(f,\psi)\text{.}\) Thus we must have \(Q(\phi,\psi)=(\bar\Delta\psi,\phi)=(f,\psi)\text{,}\) and \(\phi\in {\mathcal Dom}(\bar\Delta)\text{.}\)
