In the first case, we may assume that \(U_j\) is the unit ball of \(\mathbb R^n\text{,}\) and \({ supp}\,(\rho_j\omega)\subset U_j\text{.}\) Write

\begin{equation*} \rho_j\omega=\sum_i (-1)^i f_i dx_1\wedge\cdots \hat{dx_i}\wedge\cdots\wedge dx_n\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(\hat{}\) means omit the term. Then

\begin{equation*} \int_{U_j} d(\rho_j\omega)=\sum_{i=1}^n\frac{\pa f_i}{\pa x_i} dx_1\wedge\cdots\wedge dx_n\text{.} \end{equation*}
