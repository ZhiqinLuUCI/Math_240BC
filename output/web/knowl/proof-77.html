Proof

We know that on \(\Gamma_v\text{,}\) \(-D^2 u\geq 0\) almost everywhere. Thus we have

\begin{equation*} -a^{ij}D_{ij} u\geq n(\det A(-D^2u))^{1/n}=n\mathcal D^*\det (-D^2 u)^{1/n}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Thus we have

\begin{equation*} \det(-D^2 u)\leq\left(\frac{f^+}{n\mathcal D^*}\right)^n\text{,} \end{equation*}

and the theorem is proved.
