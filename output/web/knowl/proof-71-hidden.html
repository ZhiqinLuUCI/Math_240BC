For all \(y\in\Gamma_u\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} u(y)+p\cdot(x-y)\geq u(x),\qquad \forall x\in\Omega\text{.} \end{equation*}

We draw a ray starting from \(y\) with direction \(-p\text{.}\) Suppose the ray hits \(\pa\Omega\) at \(x_0\text{.}\) Since \(x_0-y\) is proportional to \(p\) (and in the opposite direction), we have

\begin{equation*} u(x_0)\leq u(y)+p(x_0-y)=u(y)-|x_0-y|\cdot|p|\text{.} \end{equation*}

Thus

\begin{equation*} |p|\leq\frac{2\sup|u|}{|x_0-y|}\leq\frac{2\sup|u|}{{ dist}(y,\pa\Omega)}\text{.} \end{equation*}

To prove (2), let \(F\) be a compact subset of \(\Omega\text{,}\) let \(\{p_n\}\subset\chi(F)\text{,}\) and let \(p_n\to p\) as \(n\to\infty\text{.}\) Since \(p_n\in\chi(F)\text{,}\) there is a sequence \(y_n\in F\) such that \(p_n\in\chi(y_n)\text{.}\) By definition, we have

\begin{equation*} u(y_n)+p_n(x-y_n)\geq u(x),\qquad\forall x\in\Omega \end{equation*}

By passing the subsequence if necessary, we may assume that \(p_n\to p_0, y_n\to y_0\text{.}\) Then we have \(p_0\in\chi(y_0)\text{.}\)
