Similar to the previous theorem, we can work on a torus \(T\text{.}\) Assume that \(\{ u_k\}\) is a bounded sequence of \(\mathcal H^{p}_s(T)\text{.}\) We wish to prove that a subsequence of \(\{u_k\}\) will converge in the \(|| ||_r\) norm.

Let \(\{(u_k)_\xi\}\) be the Fourier coefficients of \(u_k\) for \(k\geq 1\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation} \sum_{\xi\in \mathbb Z^{n}} (1+||\xi||^2)^s |(u_k)_\xi|^2\leq C\tag{7.5.1} \end{equation}

for some constant \(C\) independent of \(k\text{.}\) For any fixed \(\xi\text{,}\) a subsequence of \((1+||\xi||^2)^{s/2} (u_k)_\xi\) converges. By the standard diagonalization, we can find a subsequence of \(\{u_k\}\text{,}\) which we still denote as \(\{u_k\}\) for abusing of notations, such that for each \(\xi\text{,}\) \((1+|| \xi||^2)^{s/2} (u_k)_\xi\) is convergent.

We prove that \(\{u_k\}\) is a Cauchy sequence under the normal \(||||_r\text{.}\) For any \(\eps>0\text{,}\) we choose \(R\) large so that

\begin{equation} \frac{1}{(1+R^2)^{s-r}}\lt \eps\text{.}\tag{7.5.2} \end{equation}

We consider all \(\xi\) with \(||\xi||\leq R\text{.}\) There are only finitely many such \(\xi\)'s. By the assumption on the sequence \(\{u_k\}\text{,}\) there is an \(N>0\text{,}\) such that

\begin{equation*} \sum_{||\xi||\leq R} (1+||\xi||^2)^r |(u_k)_\xi-(u_l)_\xi|^2\lt \eps \end{equation*}

for \(k,l>N\text{.}\) Thus we have

\begin{equation*} ||u_k-u_l||_r^2\leq\eps+\sum_{||\xi||>R} (1+||\xi||^2)^r |(u_k)_\xi-(u_l)_\xi|^2\text{.} \end{equation*}

Using (7.5.1) and  (7.5.2), we have

\begin{equation*} ||u_k-u_l||^2_s\lt (1+2C)\eps\text{,} \end{equation*}

and the theorem is proved.
