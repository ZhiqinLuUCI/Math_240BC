Proof

Let

\begin{equation*} u_0 = \sum a _i f _i (x)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Then

\begin{equation*} u ( t, x) = \sum a_i e ^{(-a_{i} - \lambda _{1} )t} f_i (x) \end{equation*}

solves the flow equation. Obviously,

\begin{equation*} \lim_{t \rightarrow \infty} u(t, x) = a _i f_1 (x)\text{.} \end{equation*}

If we choose the \(u_0\) such that \(a _i \neq 0\text{.}\) Then the flow converges to the first eigenfunction.
