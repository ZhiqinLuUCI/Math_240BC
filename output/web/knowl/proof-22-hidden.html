We set \(x = y\) and \(k = 0\) in the above theorem. Then we have

\begin{equation*} H(x, x, t) \leq C(n) V^{-1} _x (\sqrt{t})\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since

\begin{equation*} H(x, y, t)= \sum^\infty_{k=1} e ^{-\lambda k t} f_k (x) f _k (y) \end{equation*}

we have

\begin{equation*} H(x, x, t)= \sum^\infty_{k=1} e ^{-\lambda _{k} t}\text{.} \end{equation*}

As a result we have

\begin{equation*} \sum^\infty_{k=1} e ^{-\lambda k t} \leq C(n) \int_M V^{-1} _x (\sqrt{t}) \, dx\text{.} \end{equation*}

If \(\sqrt{t} \geq d\text{,}\) then \(V^{-1} _x (\sqrt{x}) = { vol} (M)\text{,}\) if \(\sqrt{t} \leq d\text{,}\) then by the Bishop volume comparison theorem

\begin{equation*} \frac{V_x (\sqrt{t}}{ V _x (d)} \geq \left( \frac{\sqrt{t}}{d}\right) ^n\text{.} \end{equation*}

Thus

\begin{equation} \sum^\infty_{k=1} e ^{-\lambda _{k} t} \leq C(n)\left\{ \begin{array}{ll} \left(\frac{d}{\sqrt{t}}\right) ^n \amp t \leq d^2\\ 1 \amp t > d^2 \end{array} \right. .\tag{$\star$}\tag{4.1.1} \end{equation}

For fixed \(k\text{,}\) by the monotonicity, we have

\begin{equation*} ke ^{-\lambda _{k} t} \leq C(n)\left\{ \begin{array}{ll} \left(\frac{d}{\sqrt{t}}\right) ^n \amp t \leq d^2\\ 1 \amp t > d^2 \end{array} \right.\text{.} \end{equation*}

We let

\begin{equation*} \sqrt{t} = k ^{-\frac{1}{n}} d \end{equation*}

and the result follows.

We can compare the above eigenvalue estimate with the result of Cheng-Li.

From (\(\star\)), we can get

\begin{equation*} \sum^\infty_{k=1} e ^{- \lambda _{k} t} \leq C t ^{-\frac{n}{2}}\;\; t \leq d^2 \end{equation*}

where \(C\) is the absolute \(d\) constant. On the other hand, if we consider

where \(C\) is the Sobolev constant. Since for any \(t > 0\)
