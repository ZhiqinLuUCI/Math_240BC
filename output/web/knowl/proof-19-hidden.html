If \(0\neq\lambda\in\sigma_p(\Delta)\text{,}\) then for any \(\eps>0\text{,}\) there exists \(f\neq 0\) smooth with compact support, such that

\begin{equation*} \|\Delta f-\lambda f\|_{L^2}\leq\eps\|f\|_{L^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}

We then have

\begin{equation*} \left|\|df\|^2_{L^2}+\|\delta f\|^2_{L^2}-\lambda\|f\|^2_{L^2}\right|=|(f,\Delta f-\lambda f)|\leq\eps \|f\|_{L^2}^2\text{.} \end{equation*}

In particular, if \(\eps\) is small enough, we must have either

\begin{equation*} \|df\|^2_{L^2}\geq\frac\lambda 4\|f\|^2_{L^2}\text{,} \end{equation*}

or

\begin{equation*} \|\delta f\|^2_{L^2}\geq\frac\lambda 4\|f\|^2_{L^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}

For the rest of the proof, we assume that the former is correct. Using our new Weyl Criterion, we need to prove

\begin{equation*} (df, (\Delta-\lambda) df)|\leq\eps\|f\|^2_{L^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}

But this follows from

\begin{equation*} |(\delta df, (\Delta-\lambda)f)|\leq\|(\Delta-\lambda)f\|_{L^2}\cdot\|\delta df\|_{L^2}\text{,} \end{equation*}

and the fact that

\begin{equation*} \int_M|\Delta f|^2=\int_M |df|^2+\int_M|\delta f|^2\geq \int_M | df|^2\text{.} \end{equation*}

Similarly, we can prove that for function \(g(t)=(t+1)^{-1}\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} |(g(\Delta)(\Delta-\lambda) df, (\Delta-\lambda) df)|\leq\eps\|df\|^2_{L^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}
