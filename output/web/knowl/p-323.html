We shall prove that the above inequality is true even at singular points of \(\rho\text{,}\) in the sense of distribution. To see this, we let \(\Omega\) be the domain in \(X\) such that \(\rho\) is smooth on \(\Omega\text{.}\) \(\Omega\) is a star-like domain in \(X\text{.}\) Apparently

\begin{equation*} X = \Omega \cup C u t (p) \end{equation*}

where \(Cut(p)\) is the cut-locus of \(X\text{.}\) Since \(\rho\) is at least continuous, and since the measure of \(Cut (p)\) is zero, we must have

\begin{equation*} \int _X \rho \Delta \phi = \int _{\Omega} \rho \Delta \phi \end{equation*}

for any smooth function \(\phi\text{.}\) Let \(\Omega _\varepsilon\) be an exhaustion of \(\Omega\) in the sense that \(\Omega _\varepsilon \subset \Omega\text{.}\) \(\Omega _\varepsilon \subset \Omega _{\varepsilon^{\prime}}\) if \(\varepsilon > \varepsilon ^\prime\text{,}\) and

\begin{equation*} \lim_{\varepsilon \rightarrow 0} \Omega _\varepsilon = \Omega\text{.} \end{equation*}
