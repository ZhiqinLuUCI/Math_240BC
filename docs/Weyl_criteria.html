Section 2.4 The new Weyl criteria

Let \(\mathfrak X\) be Hilbert space. Let \(\mathscr B(\mathbb C)\) and let \(\mathcal P(X)\) be the space of self-adjoint projection operators. A spectral measure is a function

\begin{equation*} E: \mathscr(\mathbb C)\to \mathcal (X) \end{equation*}

satisfying the following properties

  1. \(E(\emptyset)=0, E(\mathbb C)=I\text{;}\)

  2. \(E(\bigcup B_n)=\sum E(B_n)\) for disjoint Borel sets \(\{B_n\}\text{.}\)

Let \(f\) be a measurable function and let \(x,y\in X\text{.}\) Define the operator

\begin{equation*} A=\int_{-\infty}^\infty f\,dE \end{equation*}

such that

\begin{equation*} (Ax,y)=\int_{-\infty}^\infty f \,dE_{(x,y)}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Let¬†\(H\) be a self-adjoint operator on a Hilbert space \(X\text{.}\) The norm and inner product in \(X\) are respectively denoted by¬†\(\|\cdot\|\) and \((\cdot,\cdot)\text{.}\) Let \(\sigma(H), \sigma_\mathrm{ess}(H)\) be the spectrum and the essential spectrum of \(H\text{,}\) respectively. Let \({ Dom}\,(H)\) be the domain of \(H\text{.}\) The Classical Weyl criterion states that

Theorem 2.4.1. Classical Weyl's criterion.

A nonnegative real number¬†\(\lambda\) belongs to \(\sigma(H)\) if, and only if, there exists a sequence \(\{\psi_n\}_{n \in \mathbb N} \subset { Dom}(H)\) such that

  1. \(\forall n\in\mathbb N, \|\psi_n\|=1\) ,

  2. \((H-\lambda)\psi_n \to 0, \text{ as } n\to\infty \text{ in } \mathcal H\text{.}\)

Moreover, \(\lambda\) belongs to \(\sigma_\mathrm{ess}(H)\) of \(H\) if, and only if, in addition to the above properties

  1. \(\psi_n \to 0 \text{ weakly as } n\to\infty \text{ in } \mathcal H\text{.}\)

We have the following functional analytic result, which generalizes the weak Weyl criterion. To the authors' knowledge, this result seems to be new.

Theorem 2.4.2. Charalambous-Lu.

Let \(f\) be a bounded positive continuous function over \([0,\infty)\text{.}\) A nonnegative real number \(\lambda\) belongs to the spectrum \(\sigma(H)\) if, and only if, there exists a sequence \(\{\psi_n\}_{n \in \mathbb N} \subset { Dom}(H)\) such that

  1. \(\forall n\in\mathbb N, \|\psi_n\|=1\) ,

  2. \(\displaystyle (f(H) (H-\lambda)\psi_n, (H-\lambda)\psi_n)\to 0, \text{ as } n\to\infty {and}\)

  3. \((\psi_n, (H-\lambda)\psi_n) \to 0, \text{ as } n\to\infty\text{.}\)

Moreover, \(\lambda\) belongs to \(\sigma_\mathrm{ess}(H)\) of \(H\) if, and only if, in addition to the above properties

  1. \(\psi_n \to 0, \text{ weakly as } n\to\infty\) \text{ in } \(\mathcal H\text{.}\)

Proof.

Since \(H\) is a densely defined self-adjoint operator, the spectral measure \(E\) exists and we can write

\begin{equation} H=\int_0^\infty \lambda\, dE\text{.}\tag{2.4.1} \end{equation}

Assume that \(\lambda\in\sigma(H)\text{.}\) Then by Weyl's criterion, there exists a sequence \(\{\psi_n\}\) such that

\begin{equation*} \|(H-\lambda)\psi_n\|\to 0, \|\psi_n\|=1 \end{equation*}

as \(n\to\infty\text{.}\)

We write

\begin{equation*} \psi_n=\int_0^\infty d E(t)\psi_n \end{equation*}

as its spectral decomposition. Then

\begin{equation*} (f(H) (H-\lambda)\psi_n, (H-\lambda)\psi_n)=\int_0^\infty f(t)(t-\lambda)^2 d\|E(t)\psi_n\|^2\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since \(f\) is a bounded positive function, we have

\begin{equation*} (f(H) (H-\lambda)\psi_n, (H-\lambda)\psi_n)\leq C\int_0^\infty (t-\lambda)^2 d\|E(t)\psi_n\|^2=C\|(H-\lambda)\psi_n\|^2\text{.} \end{equation*}

Moreover,

\begin{equation*} (\psi_n, (H-\lambda)\psi_n)\leq C\,\|\psi_n\|\cdot\|(H-\lambda)\psi_n\|\text{.} \end{equation*}

Thus the necessary part of the theorem is proved.

Now assume that \(\lambda>0\) and \(\lambda\notin\sigma(H)\text{.}\) Then there is a \(\lambda>\eps>0\) such that \(E(\lambda+\eps)-E(\lambda-\eps)=0\text{.}\) We write

\begin{equation} \psi_n=\psi_n^1+\psi_n^2\text{,}\tag{2.4.2} \end{equation}

where

\begin{equation*} \psi_n^1=\int_0^{\lambda-\eps} dE(t)\psi_n\text{,} \end{equation*}

and \(\psi_n^2=\psi_n-\psi_n^1\text{.}\)

Then

\begin{align*} \amp (f(H) (H-\lambda)\psi_n, (H-\lambda)\psi_n)\\ \amp =(f(H) (H-\lambda)\psi_n^1, (H-\lambda)\psi_n^1) +(f(H) (H-\lambda)\psi^2_n, (H-\lambda)\psi_n^2)\\ \amp \geq c_1\|\psi_n^1\|^2+(f(H) (H-\lambda)\psi^2_n, (H-\lambda)\psi_n^2)\geq c_1\|\psi_n^1\|^2\text{,} \end{align*}

where the positive number \(c_1\) is the infimum of the function \(f(t)(t-\lambda)^2\) on \([0,\lambda-\eps]\text{.}\) Therefore

\begin{equation*} \|\psi_n^1\|\to 0 \end{equation*}

by (2). On the other hand, we similarly get

\begin{equation*} (\psi_n, (H-\lambda)\psi_n)\geq \eps\|\psi_n^2\|^2-\lambda\|\psi_n^1\|^2\text{.} \end{equation*}

If the criteria (2), (3) are satisfied, then, by the two {estimates} above, we conclude that both \(\psi_n^1, \psi_n^2\) go to zero. This contradicts \(\|\psi_n\|=1\text{,}\) and the theorem is proved.

Note that for \(\lambda=0\text{,}\) \(\psi_n^1\) is automatically zero, and the second half of the proof would give the contradiction.

Theorem 2.4.3. Charalambous-Lu.

Let \(\sigma_p(M)\) be the spectrum of the Laplacian on \(p\)-forms. If \(\lambda\in\sigma_p(\Delta)\) and \(\lambda\neq 0\text{,}\) then \(\lambda\in\sigma_{p-1}(\Delta)\) or \(\lambda \in\sigma_{p+1}(\Delta)\text{.}\)

Remark 2.4.4.

If \(M\) is a compact manifold, then the result is trivially true: if \(f\neq 0\) is the eigenform of the eigenvalue \(\lambda\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \Delta f-\lambda f=0\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since \(d\Delta=\Delta d\text{,}\) \(\delta\Delta=\Delta\delta\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} \Delta df-\lambda df=0, \Delta\delta f-\lambda \delta f=0\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since \(\lambda\neq 0\text{,}\) we have either \(df\neq 0\text{,}\) or \(\delta f\neq 0\text{.}\) The result follows.

Proof.

If \(0\neq\lambda\in\sigma_p(\Delta)\text{,}\) then for any \(\eps>0\text{,}\) there exists \(f\neq 0\) smooth with compact support, such that

\begin{equation*} \|\Delta f-\lambda f\|_{L^2}\leq\eps\|f\|_{L^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}

We then have

\begin{equation*} \left|\|df\|^2_{L^2}+\|\delta f\|^2_{L^2}-\lambda\|f\|^2_{L^2}\right|=|(f,\Delta f-\lambda f)|\leq\eps \|f\|_{L^2}^2\text{.} \end{equation*}

In particular, if \(\eps\) is small enough, we must have either

\begin{equation*} \|df\|^2_{L^2}\geq\frac\lambda 4\|f\|^2_{L^2}\text{,} \end{equation*}

or

\begin{equation*} \|\delta f\|^2_{L^2}\geq\frac\lambda 4\|f\|^2_{L^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}

For the rest of the proof, we assume that the former is correct. Using our new Weyl Criterion, we need to prove

\begin{equation*} (df, (\Delta-\lambda) df)|\leq\eps\|f\|^2_{L^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}

But this follows from

\begin{equation*} |(\delta df, (\Delta-\lambda)f)|\leq\|(\Delta-\lambda)f\|_{L^2}\cdot\|\delta df\|_{L^2}\text{,} \end{equation*}

and the fact that

\begin{equation*} \int_M|\Delta f|^2=\int_M |df|^2+\int_M|\delta f|^2\geq \int_M | df|^2\text{.} \end{equation*}

Similarly, we can prove that for function \(g(t)=(t+1)^{-1}\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} |(g(\Delta)(\Delta-\lambda) df, (\Delta-\lambda) df)|\leq\eps\|df\|^2_{L^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Corollary 2.4.5.

Let \(X\) be a K√§hler manifold and let \(L\to X\) be a positive line bundle. Let \(\Delta_0\text{,}\) \(\Delta_1\) be the Laplacians of \(L^m\)-valued sections and \((0,1)\)-forms, respectively. We assume that both of them are self-adjoint. Then

\begin{equation*} \sigma_0(\Delta_0)\subset \{0\}\cup\sigma_1(\Delta_1)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Moreover, if \(0\in\sigma_0(\Delta_0)\text{,}\) then holomorphic sections exists.
