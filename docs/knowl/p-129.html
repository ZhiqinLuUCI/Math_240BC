Second, like most differential operators, the Laplace operator is a closed-graph operator. That is, if \(\eta_j\to \eta\) and \(\Delta\eta_j\to\eta'\) in \(L^2\) and \(\eta\in\Lambda^p(M)\text{,}\) then we must have \(\eta'=\Delta\eta\text{.}\) To see this, we consider any smooth \(p\) form \(\omega\text{:}\) We have

\begin{equation*} \int\langle\eta'-\Delta\eta,\omega\rangle=\lim_{j\to\infty}\int \langle\Delta\eta_j,\omega\rangle-\lim_{j\to\infty}\int\langle\eta_j,\Delta\omega\rangle=0\text{.} \end{equation*}
