Theorem 9.2.14. Aleksandrov maximum principle.

Let \(u\in \mathcal C(\bar\Omega)\cap W_{{ loc}}^{2,n}(\Omega)\) such that

\begin{equation*} Lu\leq f\text{,} \end{equation*}

where

\begin{equation*} Lu=-a^{ij}D_{ij} u+b^i D_i u+cu=f\text{.} \end{equation*}

Assume that

\begin{align*} \amp (a^{ij})\geq 0\qquad \text{ on } \Omega\\ \amp \sum_i\left|\left|\frac{b^i}{\mathcal D^*}\right|\right|_{L^n(\Omega)}\leq\beta\\ \amp c\geq 0 \end{align*}

Then

\begin{equation*} \sup_\Omega u\leq\sup_{\pa\Omega} u^++C\left|\left|\frac{b^i}{\mathcal D^*}\right|\right|_{L^n(\Gamma_u)}\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(C\) only depends on \(n,\beta\) and \({ diam}\,\Omega\text{.}\)
