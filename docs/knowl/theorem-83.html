Theorem 7.4.1. real Hodge Theorem.

Let \(M\) be a compact orientable Riemannian manifold. Let \(p\geq 0\text{.}\) Define

\begin{equation*} \mathcal H^p(M)=\{\phi\in\Lambda^p(M))\mid \Delta\phi=0\}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Then

  1. \(\dim \mathcal H^p(M)\lt +\infty\text{;}\)

  2. Let \(\eta\) be an arbitrary smooth \(p\)-form. Then we have

    \begin{equation*} \eta=\eta_0+d\eta_1+\delta\eta_2\text{,} \end{equation*}

    where \(\eta_0\) is a harmonic \(p\)-form; \(\eta_1\) and \(\eta_2\) are \((p-1)\) and \((p+1)\) forms, respectively,

  3. \(H^p_{DR}(M)=\mathcal H^p(M)\) for \(p\geq 0\text{.}\)
