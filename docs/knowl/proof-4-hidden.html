We have

\begin{equation*} \nabla\frac{\pa}{\pa x_i}=\Gamma_{ki}^j\frac{\pa}{\pa x_j}\otimes dx_k\text{,} \end{equation*}

and

\begin{align*} \amp \qquad \nabla^2\frac{\pa}{\pa x_i}=\frac{\pa \Gamma_{ki}^j}{\pa x_\ell}\cdot\frac{\pa}{\pa x_j}\otimes dx_k\otimes dx_\ell\\ \amp +\Gamma_{ki}^j\Gamma_{\ell j}^m\frac{\pa}{\pa x_m}\otimes dx_k\otimes dx_\ell -\Gamma_{ki}^j\Gamma_{\ell m}^k\frac{\pa}{\pa x_j} \otimes dx_m\otimes dx_\ell\text{.} \end{align*}

Thus we have

\begin{align*} \amp \qquad d_{\nabla^2}\frac{\pa}{\pa x_i}=\frac{\pa \Gamma_{ki}^j}{\pa x_\ell}\cdot\frac{\pa}{\pa x_j}\otimes dx_k\wedge dx_\ell\\ \amp +\Gamma_{ki}^j\Gamma_{\ell j}^m\frac{\pa}{\pa x_m}\otimes dx_k\wedge dx_\ell -\Gamma_{ki}^j\Gamma_{\ell m}^k\frac{\pa}{\pa x_j} \otimes dx_m\wedge dx_\ell\text{.} \end{align*}

Obviously, the last term of the above equation is zero. Thus after changing indices, we have

\begin{equation*} d_{\nabla^2}\frac{\pa}{\pa x_i}=\left(\frac{\pa \Gamma_{ki}^j}{\pa x_\ell}-\Gamma_{ki}^m\,\Gamma_{\ell m}^j\right) \cdot\frac{\pa}{\pa x_j}\otimes dx_k\wedge dx_\ell\text{.} \end{equation*}

By a straightforward computation, we get

\begin{equation*} d_{\nabla^2}\frac{\pa}{\pa x_i}=-\frac 12 R_{imk\ell}g^{mj}\,\frac{\pa}{\pa x_k}\otimes dx_k\wedge dx_\ell\text{,} \end{equation*}

where

\begin{equation*} R_{ijk\ell}=-g_{js}\left(\frac{\pa \Gamma_{ki}^s}{\pa x_\ell}-\frac{\pa \Gamma_{\ell i}^s}{\pa x_k}-\Gamma_{ki}^m\,\Gamma_{\ell m}^s+\Gamma_{\ell i}^m\,\Gamma_{k m}^s \right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
