Proof

If \(|K_0\cap \Gamma|>\delta|K_0|\text{,}\) then \(\Gamma_\delta=K_0\text{.}\) Thus if \(\Gamma_\delta\neq K_0\text{,}\) we must have

\begin{equation*} |K_0\cap\Gamma|\leq\delta|K_0|\text{.} \end{equation*}

Dividing \(K_0\) into \(2^n\) congruent sub-cubes and denoting them as \(\{K(i_1)\}_{i_1=1}^{2^n}\text{.}\) On each \(K(i_1)\text{,}\) we have either of the following

  1. \(|\Gamma\cap K(i_1)|\leq\delta|K(i_1)|\text{;}\)

  2. \(|\Gamma\cap K(i_1)|>\delta|K(i_1)|\text{.}\)

Let the collection of \(K(i_1)\) satisfying (2) be \(\mathcal F_1\text{.}\) If \(K(i_1)\) satisfies (1), continue the subdivision such that

\begin{equation*} K(i_1)=\bigcup_{i_2=1}^{2^n}K(i_1,i_2)\text{.} \end{equation*}

For each \(K(i_1,i_2)\text{,}\) we still have two cases. We let \(\mathcal F_2\) be the collection of all \(K(i_1,i_2)\) satisfying (2). On the other hand, if \(K(i_1,i_2)\) satisfying (1), continue the subdivision. Thus we have the sets \(\mathcal F_1,\cdots,\mathcal F_m,\cdots\text{.}\) Let

\begin{equation*} \mathcal F=\{K(i_1,\cdots,i_{m-1})\mid K(i_1,\cdots,i_{m-1}, i_m)\in\mathcal F_m\}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Thus if \(K(i_1,\cdots,i_m)\in\mathcal F_m\text{,}\) then

\begin{align*} \amp |K(i_1,\cdots,i_m)\cap\Gamma|>\delta|K(i_1,\cdots,i_m)|;\\ \amp |K(i_1,\cdots,i_{m-1})\cap\Gamma|\leq \delta |K(i_1,\cdots,i_{m-1})|\text{.} \end{align*}

By the definition of \(\Gamma_\delta\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} K(i_1,\cdots,i_{m-1})\subset\Gamma_\delta\text{.} \end{equation*}

Let

\begin{equation*} \tilde\Gamma_\delta=\bigcup_{K\in\mathcal F} K\subset\Gamma_\delta\text{.} \end{equation*}

Then we have

\begin{equation*} |\tilde\Gamma_\delta\cap\Gamma|=\sum_{K\in\mathcal F}|K\cap \Gamma|\leq\delta\sum_{K\in\mathcal F}|K|=\delta|\tilde\Gamma_\delta|\leq\delta|\Gamma_\delta|\text{.} \end{equation*}

On the other hand, by the definition of \(\tilde\Gamma_\delta\text{,}\) since \(\Gamma\) is measurable, by the Lebesgue Theorem, we know that for almost every point of \(\Gamma\) is dense. Thus we have

\begin{equation*} |\Gamma|=|\Gamma\cap\tilde\Gamma_\delta|\leq\delta|\Gamma_\delta|\text{.} \end{equation*}
