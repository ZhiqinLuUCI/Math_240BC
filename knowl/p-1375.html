We wish to find the the following form of the fundamental solution of the heat equation:

\begin{equation} U(x,y,t)\sim (4\pi t)^{-\frac n2} e^{-d^2(x,y)/4t}\left\{\sum_{i\geq 0}\phi_i(x,y) t^i\right\}\tag{8.2.1} \end{equation}

where \(d(x,y)\) is the distance function on the Riemannian manifold. \(U(x,y,t)\) should satisfy

  1. \(\limt U(x,y,t)=\delta_x(y)\text{,}\) where \(\delta_x(y)\) is the Dirac function at \(x\text{;}\)

  2. For any \(N\text{,}\) \(\limt (\Delta-\frac{\pa}{\pa t})U(x,y,t)=O(t^N)\text{.}\)
