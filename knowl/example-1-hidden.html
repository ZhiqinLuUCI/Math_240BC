On \(\R^n\text{,}\) we give the Riemannian metric to be

\begin{equation*} ds^2=\frac{dx_1^2+\cdots +dx_n^2}{(1+\frac K4\sum x_k^2)^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Let

\begin{equation*} A=1+\frac K4\sum x_k^2\text{.} \end{equation*}

and let \(A_j=\frac{\pa A}{\pa x_j}\text{.}\) We take

\begin{equation*} \omega_i=\frac{dx_i}{A}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Let

\begin{equation*} \omega_{ij}=\frac{1}{A}(A_i\,dx_j-A_j\,dx_i)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Then we have

\begin{equation*} d\omega_j+\omega_i\wedge\omega_{ij}=0\text{.} \end{equation*}

Moreover, we have

\begin{equation*} d\omega_{ij}+\omega_{is}\wedge\omega_{sj}=K\,\omega_i\wedge\omega_j\text{.} \end{equation*}

Thus the curvature tensor is

\begin{equation*} R_{ijk\ell}=K\,(\delta_{ik}\delta_{j\ell}-\delta_{i\ell}\delta_{jk})\text{,} \end{equation*}

and the sectional curvature is \(K\text{.}\)
