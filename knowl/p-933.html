Let \(M\) be an \(m\)-dimensional domain in \(\mathbb{R}^m\text{.}\) Let \(M\) be star-shaped. Let \(R\) be the radius of the largest ball centered at \(p\in M\) contained in \(M\) and let \(R_0\) be the smallest ball centered at \(p\) containing \(M\text{.}\) Then there is a constant \(C\text{,}\) depending only on \(m\text{,}\) such that

\begin{equation*} \eta_1 \geqslant C\frac{R^m}{R_0^{m+2}} \end{equation*}
